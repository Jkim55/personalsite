Learning to Code

Where I've Been

The path I travelled to pursue programming is kind of a meandering one. I started off by studying Economics as an undergrad and working in Investment Management after graduation. While I loved working with my clients and studying the economic forces that drive the market, I found this career to be a bit too dry and I needed to be more creative. So, I searched something that would allow me to engage my creative side more and I eventually landed on interior design. As a designer, I found that I loved the creative problem solving aspects of the work. However, the day-to-day was not particularly challenging and I needed something that was going to challenge me while engaging both my creative and analytical sides. So, I searched again and landed on programming, which is most definitely challenging, creative and analytical. A perfect fit!

Where I'm Currently

I'm just a few days away from starting the Full Stack Immersive (FSI) program at Galvanize and I'm so excited. At this point, I have a bit of experience with Ruby, a faint familiarity of Javascript, and no experience with CSS/HTML outside of the gSchool prework. So, I consider myself a complete beginner at the start of the program. I have a lot to learn and thrilled to build my knowledge base with fellow students!

I don't know .js, but let's goooooooooo-ooo!

Where I'll Go

I'm passionate about contributing to causes that empower underrepresented communities. In my opinion, education is such a pivotal instrument of empowerment. So, the lack of quality and barriers to access of resources in primary and seconday education within certain communities really concerns me. As a developer, I'd like to be involved in contributing toward solutions that can be that enhance students' lives and create an impactful change. Therefore, upon finishing the FSI program, I plan to target EdTech companies in my job search. I also plan to get involved with organizations, like CodeNOW and Girl Develop It, where I can mentor youths, particularly girls, who are interested in programming.
